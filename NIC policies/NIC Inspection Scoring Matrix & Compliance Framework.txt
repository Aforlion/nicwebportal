

# 1. NATIONAL INSTITUTE OF CAREGIVERS (NIC)

## INSPECTION SCORING MATRIX & COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

**Effective Date:** [Insert Date]
**Issued by:** National Institute of Caregivers (NIC)

---

## 1. PURPOSE

This Inspection Scoring Matrix establishes a standardized method for evaluating care facilities seeking NIC accreditation or renewal.

It ensures:

* Objectivity
* Transparency
* Consistency
* Defensibility of accreditation decisions

---

## 2. INSPECTION METHODOLOGY

Inspections are conducted through:

* Document review
* Physical facility assessment
* Staff interviews
* Observation of care practices
* Records verification

Each inspection area is scored independently.

---

## 3. SCORING STRUCTURE

* **Total Possible Score:** 100 points
* Each category carries a weighted score
* Critical failures may result in automatic non-compliance regardless of total score

---

## 4. INSPECTION CATEGORIES & WEIGHTING

### A. Legal & Regulatory Compliance – **15 Points**

* Valid CAC registration
* Operational permits
* Compliance with applicable laws
* Required documentation availability

**Critical Failure:** Operating illegally or with falsified documents

---

### B. Governance & Management – **10 Points**

* Defined management structure
* Written policies and procedures
* Accountability mechanisms
* Complaints handling system

---

### C. Human Resources & Staffing – **20 Points**

* Qualified caregivers
* NIC certification or equivalent
* Staffing ratios
* Supervision and training records
* Background checks

**Critical Failure:** Unqualified or abusive staff

---

### D. Care Standards & Service Delivery – **20 Points**

* Individual care plans
* Medication management
* Hygiene and dignity practices
* Nutrition and feeding standards
* Emergency response protocols

**Critical Failure:** Neglect, abuse, or unsafe care practices

---

### E. Health, Safety & Environment – **15 Points**

* Cleanliness and sanitation
* Fire safety
* Infection prevention
* Accessibility
* Secure premises

---

### F. Facilities & Infrastructure – **10 Points**

* Sleeping arrangements
* Toilets and bathing facilities
* Common areas
* Medical and first aid equipment

---

### G. Record Keeping & Data Management – **10 Points**

* Client records
* Staff duty rosters
* Incident logs
* Medication records

---

## 5. COMPLIANCE RATINGS

| Score Range  | Status                  |
| ------------ | ----------------------- |
| **85 – 100** | Fully Compliant         |
| **70 – 84**  | Conditionally Compliant |
| **50 – 69**  | Non-Compliant           |
| **Below 50** | Seriously Non-Compliant |

---

## 6. CONDITIONAL COMPLIANCE

Facilities rated **Conditionally Compliant** must:

* Submit a corrective action plan
* Implement improvements within a defined timeframe
* Undergo follow-up inspection

Failure to comply may result in suspension.

---

## 7. AUTOMATIC NON-COMPLIANCE

Regardless of score, the following result in immediate non-compliance:

* Abuse or neglect
* Operating without legal registration
* Obstruction of inspectors
* Falsification of records
* Serious safety hazards

---

## 8. INSPECTION REPORTING

Inspection reports shall include:

* Scores per category
* Observations and findings
* Required corrective actions
* Accreditation recommendation

Reports form part of NIC’s permanent compliance records.

---

## 9. APPEALS

Facilities may appeal inspection outcomes in accordance with NIC appeals procedures.

---

## 10. REVIEW & UPDATE

NIC reserves the right to revise this matrix to reflect evolving standards.
